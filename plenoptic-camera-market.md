<p><p>The plenoptic camera market is divided into two main types: standard plenoptic cameras and focused plenoptic cameras. Standard plenoptic cameras capture light field information, allowing for the post-capture adjustment of focus and perspective. They are primarily used in consumer photography and basic imaging applications. Focused plenoptic cameras, on the other hand, are designed for more specialized uses, such as scientific research and professional imaging, offering higher resolution and improved depth-of-field control for detailed capture and analysis.</p></p>
